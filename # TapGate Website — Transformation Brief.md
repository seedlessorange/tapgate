## PART 1 — BUSINESS CONTEXT (CRITICAL)

### Two-brand strategy

TapGate is one of **two related brands** from the same parent company:

- **TapGate** — European market, B2C focus, sold direct-to-consumer to homeowners via the web. Starting in France, expanding across EU.
- **ZoomIN** — US market, B2B focus, sold through installer and dealer networks to property managers, HOAs, and commercial buildings.

This brief is **only about TapGate**. Keep the two brand identities visually and verbally distinct. No cross-brand mentions on this site. No shared logos. No "also available in the US as ZoomIN" callouts. The two brands exist because they serve genuinely different customers through genuinely different channels.

### Who TapGate is for

- **Primary buyer:** European homeowners (starting France) with an automated residential gate — villa owners, suburban homeowners with driveway gates, rural property owners. Tired of remote controls that break, fobs that get lost, and gate codes nobody remembers.
- **Secondary buyer:** Small rental hosts (Airbnb, short-term rentals) who need to share gate access with guests without physical key handoff.
- **Tertiary buyer:** Small multi-family buildings (2–10 units) where a single property owner manages access for a handful of residents.

### Why this brand is different from ZoomIN

| | TapGate (EU B2C) | ZoomIN (US B2B) |
|---|---|---|
| Audience | Individual homeowners | Property managers, HOAs, installers |
| Channel | Direct web purchase, credit card | Dealer network, quotes, invoiced |
| Buying cycle | Minutes to days | Weeks to months |
| Price anchor | "€249, ships free, 30-day return" | "Starting at $X, quote for volume" |
| Tone | Warm, specific, personality-forward | Professional, credentialed, technical |
| Hero message | Makes daily life simpler | Reduces operational headaches at scale |
| Key differentiator | 10-min install, share keys by text, no fobs | 25-year installer relationships, Wiegand retrofit, professional deployment |

**Do not blur these lines.** The worst mistake would be to make TapGate sound like an enterprise SaaS product or ZoomIN sound like a consumer DTC brand.

## PART 2 — LESSONS APPLIED FROM STRATEGIC ANALYSIS

The following lessons come from studying Nuki.io (the strongest European DTC smart access brand) and from the business direction becoming clearer over multiple iterations.

### Lessons that apply directly to TapGate

**1. Lead with "retrofit" as the #1 message, always.**
Nuki's most-repeated phrase on every page is "retrofittable." It answers the biggest purchase objection (*do I have to replace my gate?*) before it's asked. For TapGate, the equivalent is "works with the gate you already have." This should appear in the hero, in multiple section headers, and as an eyebrow tagline across the site.

**2. Compatibility check is a primary navigation item.**
Nuki's nav starts with "Compatibility check" — not "Products." It sends buyers to an interactive tool that reduces purchase anxiety. For TapGate, build a "Will TapGate work on my gate?" page as the first (or second) nav item with a simple 3-question quiz. This is the single highest-leverage conversion optimization we can make.

**3. Three-word benefit triplet.**
Nuki uses **Smart · Simple · Secure** — three short words, three icons, one sentence each. It reads fast on mobile and is instantly memorable. For TapGate, use **Retrofit · Instant · Shareable** as the equivalent. Put it prominently on the homepage.

**4. Bundle-first pricing.**
Nuki's prominent pricing is the bundle (€359 for Home Set Pro), not the individual product. This raises average order value and simplifies decision-making. For TapGate, design the pricing section to feature the bundle as "Most Popular," with the single-unit purchase as the alternate option.

**5. Accessory ecosystem signaling.**
Even with a lean initial SKU list, the site architecture should signal "this is a platform" by showing accessory placeholders (Fob, Keypad, Range Extender) even if marked "coming soon." Positions the brand as more than one product.

**6. Founder story / European heritage.**
Nuki prominently features "Designed in Austria, made in Europe" and video of the two founder brothers. Builds trust with European DTC buyers who are wary of generic IoT brands. For TapGate, lean on the ISN heritage (25 years in access control, family-run, engineering-led) without claiming "Made in Europe" unless it's actually true.

**7. Downloadable compatibility guide as lead magnet.**
Nuki offers a compatibility guide PDF. Useful for serious buyers, also an email capture opportunity. For TapGate, create a placeholder "TapGate Compatibility Guide" PDF with an email gate. This captures intent from buyers who aren't yet ready to click "Buy."

**8. Community / loyalty program for soft email capture.**
Nuki has a "Nuki Club" — free membership with free shipping and exclusive offers. It's an email capture wrapped in a loyalty frame. For TapGate, build a "TapGate Community" signup with free extended warranty, early product access, and engineer tips. Not launch-critical, but design for it.

**9. Payment methods as trust signals.**
Nuki shows Visa, Mastercard, PayPal, Apple Pay, Google Pay badges prominently. Important for EU DTC trust — European buyers expect multiple payment options, including SEPA and country-specific methods (Carte Bancaire in France, iDEAL in Netherlands, Bancontact in Belgium).

**10. The product page rhythm.**
Nuki's product page follows: hero → three benefits → installation callout → design callout → speed callout → all-in-one features → accessories upsell → compare products → FAQ. Long page, high confidence, lots of reinforcement before asking for the sale. For TapGate, follow this rhythm on the product page.

### Lessons that DO NOT apply to TapGate

These came up during ZoomIN planning but should be explicitly avoided on the TapGate site:

- Do not use SwarmOne-style isometric B2B system diagrams as the hero. That's an enterprise-buyer aesthetic. For consumer DTC, the hero should be a product photo, a lifestyle shot, or a simple interactive animation.
- Do not build installer-focused content. Installers are not TapGate's audience. If a homeowner wants to hire an installer, they can — but we don't sell to installers directly.
- Do not use B2B pricing framing ("Contact sales for quote"). Pricing is transparent, the price is on the page, the buy button works.
- Do not use cold professional navy + signal blue as the brand palette. That's ZoomIN's palette. TapGate stays warm teal + charcoal.
- Do not build case study pages as a first-class content type. B2C doesn't convert on case studies the way B2B does. Use testimonials instead — shorter, more emotional, less data-focused.
- Do not write in an institutional/credentialed voice. TapGate is warmer, more personality-forward, more human.

## PART 3 — BRAND FOUNDATION (REVISED)

### Company one-liner
TapGate turns your phone into your gate remote. Retrofits any residential gate in about 10 minutes. Share access by text message — no fobs, no codes, no fumbling.

### What makes TapGate different

1. **Retrofit in 10 minutes** — Connects to any gate operator via trigger terminals. Two wires. Keep the gate you already own.
2. **Phone and watch, not just phone** — iPhone, Android, Apple Watch. One tap from any of them.
3. **Share by text message** — Send a guest a link, they install the app, they're in. No gate codes to remember, no fobs to hand off.
4. **Works offline** — Pure Bluetooth. No WiFi required. No monthly fees required.
5. **Optional cloud** — €5/month adds remote management, activity logs, notifications. Skip it and TapGate still works perfectly.
6. **Family-run, 25 years in access control** — Parent company heritage from a family business that's been making access control systems since 2000.

### Brand personality
- **Warm, specific, and confident.** Write like a knowledgeable friend who happens to know gates, not a corporate brochure.
- **Allergic to jargon.** Explain Bluetooth and trigger terminals without sounding like a spec sheet.
- **Grounded, not hyped.** No "revolutionary," no "game-changing," no "seamless." Name specific problems and specific solutions.
- **Practical over clever.** Every claim is paired with a concrete detail (10 minutes, under 1 second, 20 keys, 2 wires).
- **Personality over polish.** Lines like "Hand out keys like candy" and "Ditch the remote" are GOOD. Keep this voice — it's the brand's most valuable asset.

### Voice & tone rules
- Second person ("you") when addressing the homeowner
- Sentence case for body copy. Title Case for headings. Never random mid-sentence capitalization.
- Brand name is always **TapGate** — capital T, capital G, no space, no hyphen. Never "Tapgate," "tap gate," "TAPGATE," or "TapGATE."
- Avoid these words: *seamless, cutting-edge, revolutionary, solutions (as a noun), empower, leverage, game-changer, unlock (as a metaphor), innovative, next-generation, disrupt, best-in-class.*
- Use contractions — "It's," "you'll," "we've." This is a consumer DTC brand, not a press release.
- Be concrete — "5 minutes," "two wires," "under 1 second," "30-metre range" beat vague adjectives every time.

### Visual identity

**Color palette (warm, consumer-friendly, light-first):**
- **Primary teal:** `#0E6D6B` — logo color, primary CTAs, active states, brand accents
- **Deep charcoal:** `#0F1A1A` — headings on light backgrounds, dark mode backgrounds where used
- **Off-white:** `#F5F5F2` — primary page background, product photography backdrop
- **Pure white:** `#FFFFFF` — card surfaces, clean content areas
- **Warm gray:** `#6B7280` — secondary text, captions, metadata
- **Muted teal:** `#D4E8E7` — light teal for section backgrounds, subtle accent
- **Success green:** `#10B981` — confirmation states, sparingly

**Typography:**
- **Primary:** Inter (400, 500, 600, 700) for all UI, headings, body
- **Hierarchy:** H1 56–72px desktop / 40–44px mobile; H2 40–48px; H3 24–28px; H4 18–20px; body 16–18px
- **Letter-spacing:** -0.02em for H1/H2; -0.01em for H3; 0 for body; +0.08em uppercase for eyebrows

**Logo:** Assume the TapGate logo exists at `/public/logo.svg` and `/public/logo-white.svg`. The logo is a teal fingerprint-gate mark with "TapGate" wordmark, capital T and capital G.

**Imagery style:**
- Premium product photography on clean off-white backgrounds (Eero / Ubiquiti / Nest aesthetic)
- Warm lifestyle photography at blue hour or golden hour (homeowner arriving home, hand on phone, gate opening in background)
- European architectural context — stone villa gates, French countryside driveways, Mediterranean courtyards
- Avoid: corporate stock, fingerprint imagery beyond the logo, abstract "smart home" networks, padlocks or shields

**UI patterns:**
- Generous whitespace; 80–120px vertical padding between sections
- Rounded corners: 8px inputs, 12px cards, 16px large containers, 999px (pill) buttons
- Subtle shadows: `0 1px 3px rgba(15, 26, 26, 0.06)` — nothing aggressive
- Buttons: primary teal pill with white text; secondary teal outline pill
- Hover states: subtle lift, no color flips
- Focus states: 2px teal outline, visible and accessible

## PART 6 — TECHNICAL IMPLEMENTATION

### Tech stack
- **Framework:** Next.js 14 (App Router), TypeScript, Tailwind CSS
- **Animation:** Framer Motion for page-level motion; preserve the existing JS-based interactive animations from the old site (gate toggle, install steps, key sharing)
- **E-commerce:** Stripe Checkout or Shopify Buy Button. For launch, a simple "Reserve your TapGate" form is acceptable if the store isn't ready.
- **Forms:** Formspree or similar for contact, community signup, compatibility-quiz contact
- **Analytics:** Plausible or Fathom (GDPR-friendly, no cookies)
- **Hosting:** Vercel

### Key technical requirements

1. **Localization-ready:** Structure copy in `/content/en/` so French translations can be added as `/content/fr/` later. Use `next-intl` or equivalent routing.
2. **GDPR-compliant:** Minimal cookie banner. Privacy policy. Clear consent checkboxes on all forms. No Google Analytics.
3. **Performance:** Lighthouse 95+ performance, 100 accessibility, 100 best practices, 100 SEO on every page.
4. **SEO:** Unique title / description / OG image per page. Product schema on product pages.
5. **Accessibility:** WCAG AA minimum. All animations respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
6. **Mobile-first:** Excellent at 375px width. Test animations specifically on mobile.

In `/components/`:
